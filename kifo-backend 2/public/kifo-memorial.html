Generated life story

Margaret Rose Williams was born on a warm summer morning in Chicago, Illinois, the second of five children to Thomas and Eleanor Hartley. She grew up in the Logan Square neighborhood, where she developed a lifelong love of gardening, jazz music, and feeding anyone who happened to cross the threshold of her kitchen.

She met her husband, James Williams, at a church social in 1963, and they married the following spring in a ceremony her daughters still describe as "the most beautiful thing they've ever witnessed." Together they raised four children — Patricia, James Jr., Daniel, and Susan — in the same house they bought in 1968, a brick bungalow with a garden that neighbors have admired for decades.

Margaret worked for over 30 years as a school librarian at Lincoln Elementary, where she is remembered by generations of students for reading to them with full dramatic flair, and for always having a butterscotch candy available in her cardigan pocket.

She is survived by her husband of 61 years, her four children, eleven grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren who called her "Gram-Gram." She passed peacefully at home on November 12, 2024, surrounded by her family — her garden still blooming outside the window.
